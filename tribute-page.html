Nikola Tesla

A Revolutionary Inventor

July 1856

Nikola Tesla is born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (now Croatia).

1875-1878

Enrolls at Austrian Polytechnic to study electrical engineering. Drops out without finishing his degree in December 1878.

1882

Moves to Paris and begins work as an electrical engineer with Continental Edison Company. Experiments with magnetic-field-based devices and comes up with idea for induction motor.

June 6 1884

Arrives in United States with little money and a letter from a former employer recommending him to Thomas Edison.

1885

Quits his job working for Edison due to unfair compensation for overhauling and improving the efficiency of Edison's motors.

1886

Founds Tesla Electric Light & Manufacturing Company in Rahway, New Jersey.

1887-1890

The War of Currents rages, ultimately culminating in a victory for the alternating-current model of electrical generation (over against Edison's direct-current model) developed by Tesla and championed by his associate George Westinghouse.

1887-1895

Experiments with X-Rays using his patented single terminal vacuum tubes. Nearly all his X-Ray experiments are destroyed in a fire at his lab in March 1895.

1891

Patents the Tesla coil.

July 30 1891

Gains United States citizenship status.

1893

With Westinghouse, powers the World's Columbian Exhibition in Chicago and displays the powers of alternating current for the world to see.

1899

Relocates his research to Colorado Springs, where he begins conducting experiments of higher voltage, higher frequency, and over longer distances.

1901-1905

Constructs Wardenclyffe Tower on Long Island, New York. Due to waning interest from investors and his own failing finances, Tesla ended up abandoning the project unfinished.

1913

Patents bladeless turbine engine.

1916

Files for bankruptcy.

1934

Theorizes on the possibility of inventing a "death ray" using a concentrated particle beam to project large amounts of energy over long distances.

January 7 1943

Tesla dies of heart failure. The cremated remains currently reside at the Nikola Tesla Museum in Belgrade, Yugoslavia.
